18. A well-known textile company in Indonesia asks for advice from you about the optimal price for one of its superior products. The data provided by the textile company management are as follows: Sales (units) 282.500 Price per unit $. 2.00 Marginal cost per unit $. 1,00 Price elasticity of demand -3,25 Question : a) Determine the optimal price for the superior product of the textile company, if the company is at the maximum profit level. b) Determine the price of the superior product of the textile company in order to obtain maximum sales revenue c) Explain to the management of textile companies, that the assumptions underlying your analysis about price sensitivity!​


Penjelasan:

a) To determine the optimal price for the superior product of the textile company at the maximum profit level, we need to consider the price elasticity of demand and the marginal cost per unit. The optimal price can be found using the formula:

Optimal Price = Marginal Cost / (1 + (1 / Price Elasticity of Demand))

Given:

Marginal Cost per unit = $1.00

Price Elasticity of Demand = -3.25

Substituting these values into the formula, we can calculate the optimal price:

Optimal Price = $1.00 / (1 + (1 / -3.25))

Optimal Price ≈ $1.00 / (1 - 0.3077)

Optimal Price ≈ $1.00 / 0.6923

Optimal Price ≈ $1.44

Therefore, the optimal price for the superior product of the textile company, at the maximum profit level, is approximately $1.44 per unit.

b) To determine the price of the superior product of the textile company in order to obtain maximum sales revenue, we need to find the price at the point of unitary elasticity of demand. Unitary elasticity of demand occurs when the price elasticity of demand is equal to -1. The formula to calculate the price at unitary elasticity is:

Price at Unitary Elasticity = Marginal Cost / (1 - 1 / Price Elasticity of Demand)

Using the given values, we can calculate the price at unitary elasticity:

Price at Unitary Elasticity = $1.00 / (1 - 1 / -3.25)

Price at Unitary Elasticity ≈ $1.00 / (1 + 0.3077)

Price at Unitary Elasticity ≈ $1.00 / 1.3077

Price at Unitary Elasticity ≈ $0.765

Therefore, the price of the superior product of the textile company to obtain maximum sales revenue is approximately $0.765 per unit.

c) In the analysis above, the assumption made is that the price elasticity of demand remains constant. Price elasticity of demand measures the responsiveness of demand to changes in price. In this case, the price elasticity of demand is given as -3.25, indicating that the demand for the product is relatively elastic. This means that a small change in price will have a larger impact on the quantity demanded.

It is important for the management of the textile company to understand the concept of price sensitivity and elasticity of demand. They should consider the price elasticity of demand when setting prices for their products. A highly elastic demand suggests that a decrease in price may lead to a significant increase in demand and total revenue. Conversely, an increase in price may result in a decrease in demand and total revenue.

The company should also consider the marginal cost per unit when setting the price. By setting the price slightly above the marginal cost, the company can maximize its profit.
