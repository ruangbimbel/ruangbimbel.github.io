6. Explain:What's the difference: 1) reading the lines 2) reading between the lines 3) reading beyond the lines Give some example (Jelaskan dalam bahasa indonesia juga gak apa2)


The differences are:
1. This sentence states that you're reading the lines. This might/ could mean that you're reading a specific number of lines from a text, or you might be reading the whole text. Ex.: I'm reading the lines of the article.
2. This sentence states that you're reading or finding something, a diffrent or a more accurate or simplified meaning of a text, or both, but not what the text blatantly says or not as what the text literally says. Ex.: When you need to read a really long novel or a story in a limited amount of time, you need to read between the lines to get your job done quickly. This text functions almost the same as TL;DR or as they say: To Late; Don't Read.
3. This sentence means that you're reading what's not written in something. You're thinking outside the box. Ex.: To find the real meaning of a story, you need to read between and beyond the lines. Ex.2: Cleo needs to read beyond the lines to find another solution for his problem. Ex.3: Her mom reads of what's beyond the line and found her dirty secrets of all that she did by far.

23. Was she reading these lines?( turn into passive voice )a) these lines were being readb) she was being read these linesc) Were these lines being reading by her?d) Were these lines being read by her?Yok kakak" bantu akuu​


≡ Passive Voice ≡

(d) Were these lines being read by her?

Pembahasan

Passive voice merupakan bentuk kalimat yang subjeknya adalah penerima dari suatu aksi. Sementara itu, objeknya adalah pelaku dari aksi tersebut.

based on question

Past continuous tense

av → subject + was/were + verb-ing + O/C

pv → subject + was/were + being + verb 3 + by (agent)

–» active

Was she reading these lines?

subject = (was) sheverb = readingobject = these lines

–» passive

Were these lines being read by her?

subject = (were) these linesverb = (being) readobject = her

verb on question

Irregular verbs; kata kerja tidak beraturan.

verb 1 = readverb 2 = readverb 3 = readmeaning = “membaca”

answer:

➡ Were these lines being read by her? (d)

Jawaban:

E. Below

Penjelasan:

Sebab "underneath" dan "below" mempunyai arti yang sama dalam bahasa Indonesia yaitu "dibawah".

Sedangkan untuk "above" artinya adalah "diatas" sedangkan yang ditanyakan adalah "bawah".

Untuk "over" itu kurang lebih sama seperti "above". Mempunyai arti dalam bahasa Indonesia yaitu "diatas". Hanya saja saat penggunaan "over" dan "above" berbeda.

"Between" mempunyai arti "diantara". Mobil tidak bisa berada diantara jembatan kan? Karena jembatan

biasanya dibuat untuk mobil menyebrang sungai/laut sehingga tak mungkin bagi mobil untuk berads "diantara" jembatan.

Nah, untuk "bottom" ini lebih masuk/pas untuk penggunaan kalimat seperti sesuatu yang punya "dasar" seperti botol, laut, dll.

Biasanya yang menjadi kata yang digarisbawahi itu kata yang paling mencolok dari suatu kalimat.

40. What is the difference between a TAX and a Duty or levies? (10 lines) ​


Jawaban:

Taxes, duties, and levies are all forms of financial obligations imposed by governments on individuals, businesses, or other entities. While they share some similarities, there are some key differences between these three types of financial obligations:

Penjelasan:

Taxes are generally imposed on income, profits, or the value of goods or services. They are mandatory contributions to the government, and failure to pay taxes can result in penalties or legal consequences.Duties are typically levied on the import or export of goods. They are a type of tax that is specifically applied to international trade, and they can be either ad valorem (based on the value of the goods) or specific (based on the quantity or weight of the goods).Levies are fees charged by governments for specific services or activities. They are often used to fund government programs or infrastructure projects, and they can be imposed on individuals or businesses that use these services or activities.

Overall, the main difference between taxes, duties, and levies is the type of financial obligation they represent and the context in which they are applied. Taxes are generally applied to income or the value of goods and services, while duties are specifically applied to international trade. Levies, on the other hand, are fees charged for specific government services or activities.
