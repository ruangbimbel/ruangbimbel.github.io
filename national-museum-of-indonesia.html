The text is for questions 16 to 19

Inaugurated in 1846, the Hungarian National Museum houses, preserves, and displays Hungary

most important collection of historical relics from prehistoric times to the present day. The Count Fere

Széchényi established a library in 1802 donating all his books, which was then followed by the donau

of his coin collections, transforming the library into the Hungarian National Museum.

The museum is housed in one of the most beautiful Hungarian classicist-styled palaces.

imposing structure was designed by the architect Mihály Pollack. It has a beautifully sculpted pediment

and enormous columns that will remind you of a Roman temple.

The Museum's interior is breath-taking. It is replete with marble pillars and beautiful paintings that

cover entire walls and ceilings. The various collections reflect the history of the country since it was

established to the present day. The museum houses various archaeological objects, as well as several

treasures that display Hungary's history and culture.

Some of the most interesting objects include the coronation cloak used by the Kings of Hungary, the

statue of a handmade golden deer from the sixth century BC, or a funerary crown from thirteenth

century, among many other relics. There is a Roman lapidary in the building's basement.

3. A survey was carried out on 150 tourists to Wales asking which of the following attractions, if any, they had visited: ■ National Slate Museum ■ National Wool Museum ■ National Library of Wales 27 of the visitors had visited National Slate Museum and National Wool Museum and, of these, 8 had visited all three attractions. 72 of the people had visited National Library of Wales. 70 of the people had visited National Slate Museum. Some further information is given on the Venn diagram below. How many visitors had visited the National Library of Wales only? Tolong jawab ya, makasih


Jawab:

Thirty four visitors had visited the National Library of Wales only.

Penjelasan dengan langkah-langkah:

The processes of analyze numbers of visitors that had visited the National Library of Wales only:

Select the numbers of the National Library of Wales visitors.Calculate the numbers of the National Library only visitors by eliminating the visitors who didn't visit the National Library only.Get the conclusion.

1st Process

From Diagram Venn:

Visitors that had visited National State Museum and the National Library of Wales: x1 = 20 peopleVisitors that had visited the National Wool Museum and the National Library of Wales: x2 = 10 people

From the text:

Visitors that had visited all three attractions: x3 = 8 peopleTotal visitors that had visited the National Library of Wales: xt = 72 people

2nd Process

Elements of total visitors of the National Library of Wales:

Visitors that had visited National State Museum and the National Library of Wales: x1 Visitors that had visited the National Wool Museum and the National Library of Wales: x2Visitors that had visited all three attractions: x3 Visitors that had visited the National Library of Wales only: x4

So, xt = x1+x2+x3+x4

72 people =20 people + 10 people + 8 people + x4

x4 = 72 people - 38 people

x4 = 34 people

3rd Process

So, there were 34 visitors that had visited the National Library of Wales only.
